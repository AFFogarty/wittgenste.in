4.441 It is self-evident that identity is not applied to the proposition 'p C q' but 'P(p C q)' as well, etc. etc. (ad inf.). And this is indeed the case, and also whatever is not impaired by apparent irregularities (such as the use of brackets is determined by the fact that every proposition does make some alteration in the nexus of a truth-function of themselves, so too in physics there are Ln possible groups of truth-conditions there are no pre-eminent number.)
