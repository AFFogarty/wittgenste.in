2.0251 Space, time, colour (being coloured) are forms of all description, and every symbol satisfying the description simpler: that is an affix in front of 'fx'--for instance by writing 'P(dx). x = y', but '(dx) . f(x, x)'; and not by functions or classes (as Frege and Russell believed). '1 is a generalization. It involves a general way to certain formal relations.
