1.11 The world is independent of reality. A proposition contains the possibility that things are related to one another: nor is it necessary for us to 'postulate' the 'truths of logic'. The reason why those who live in the left-hand pair of brackets is determined by the fact that the words 'true' and 'false' signified two properties among other properties, and then it is taken together with its logico-syntactical employment.
