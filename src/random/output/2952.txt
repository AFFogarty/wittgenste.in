3.31 I call any part of our everyday language, just as well, or as badly, as the law of least effort in nature, etc. etc.--all these are present, and where these are present, we already have a sign-language that excludes them by single letters ('x', 'y', 'z'). I write 'N(E)'. N(E) is the proposition how everything stands logically if it were true. Indeed, the logical properties of propositions and functions must not overlap.
