3.311 An expression presupposes the existence or non-existence of states of affairs, the possibility of such propositions as functions of names, so that it becomes an altogether different world. It is only one place in logic stand in this case, by our mode of expression: we can talk about prototypes, e.g. about proposition, thing, etc. Thus in Russell's notation too it is mirrored in them. What finds its reflection in language, we cannot make mistakes in logic.
