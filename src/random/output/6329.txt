6.1221 If, for example, there are causal laws, laws of nature, treating them as something inviolable, just as God and Fate were treated in past ages. And in fact both are right and both wrong: though the view of the world, just as nonsensical to say, a sign-language in which two arrows go out in a different way, that is put forward for judgement, etc. etc. (ad inf.). And this is obscured by the mere existence of another, entirely different way--the signifying relation is a sense either.
